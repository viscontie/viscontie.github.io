CampusCare

CampusCare

Issue to Address

Mental health and wellness resources at Carleton are not easily accessible and often fragmented.

  • Resources are spread across multiple platforms
  • Misinformation and lack of information prevalent among student body
  • Lack of navigable centralized information hub
  • Resources and important information about accessing them can get lost in current campus websites

Our Goal

Ensure every Carleton student has easy access to all wellness resources within our campus and local community.

Low Fidelity Prototype

My app design addresses these issues by including...

  • Resources Page: Laying out all mental health-related organizations and resources available to Carleton students and where to access them.
  • Fast FAQs: Aiming to combat misinformation and lack of information by answering commonly asked questions like transportation to appointments off-campus, how to make counseling appointments, or use school insurance.
  • Wellness Calendar: Allows users to select upcoming dates and see events related to mental health and well-being happening that day.

The UX Research Process

In an effort to create an app that best serves the needs of Carleton students, we engaged in the process of user-centered design. After developing a requirement elicitation protocol for our stakeholders (students and staff), we performed 5 semi-structured interviews and two participatory design interviews through fictional inquiry. To ensure we obtained the most complete picture of the needs of students, we selected student and staff stakeholders of diverse backgrounds. This included the director of counseling at Carleton, the director of International Student Life, and students of different majors, class years, countries, and a range of familiarity with mental health resources on campus. Through this process, we gathered critical insight into common misconceptions, expectations, desires, and knowledge of our stakeholders.

In our interviews, all students voiced a desire for a centralized list of mental health and wellness resources available to students.

Research Analysis

After completing our interviews, we began the process of generating the requirements for designing our interface using the axial coding process. After an initial axial coding of a few interviews, each group member added their codes to our affinity diagram. From here, we collaborated as a group to develop a complete codebook that included all axial codes under the subcategories of phenomena, causal conditions, contextual conditions, intervening conditions, action/interactional strategies, and consequences. From here, two teammates axial coded each transcription before collaborating as a group to come to a final consensus on the coded transcripts and analyze the completed codebook to gather what codes were more prevalent, i.e., what concepts were most common amongst stakeholders.

2nd Prototype

Requirement 1: Counseling Options

Comprehensive list of what counseling services are available, how to access/schedule them, and other relevant information

Throughout our interviews, stakeholders voiced a desire for immediate and clear information about the various forms of counseling available on campus and how to access it. Furthermore, many stakeholders were not aware or had wrong information about how to access counseling. Therefore, this prototype:

  • Lays out the various forms of counseling so that users can click the kind they are interested in learning more about right from opening the app.
  • Responds to misconceptions and questions stakeholders often had in interviews on the Fast FAQs page.
  • Provides users with the scheduling page for counseling at Student Health and Counseling, how to install TimelyCare, how to join mental health collectives, and a list of off-campus counseling services.

Requirement 2: Common Q&As

List of commonly asked questions and misconceptions with detailed answers below each based on the user-selected question

Many students are unaware or have the wrong information about how to access resources like counseling, physical check-out resources, off-campus health and transportation, contacting the dean and case management, etc.

During interviews with student and staff stakeholders, it became apparent that many students either lack information about available resources or receive unintentional misinformation from misconceptions of other students. Furthermore, some staff voiced the challenge of getting information out to students. Thus, to combat this, this prototype includes:

  • A Fast FAQs page that lists the most common questions and misconceptions students have.
  • Users may select a question and receive a comprehensive list of transportation options without having to navigate multiple web pages, bus schedules, or receive wrong information from fellow students.

Requirement 3: Resource Directory

Comprehensive guide of all mental health and wellness resource options with links to all related organizations at Carleton and Student Wellness Directory

To address the fragmented and scattered info about mental health between departments and organizations at Carleton, this prototype allows users to:

  • Access a list of all organizations on campus that provide mental health and wellness resources.
  • Select the desired organization to view basic information on what resources are provided and how to access them.
  • Get direct links to webpages for all organizations.

UX Evaluation

For the UX evaluation stage of the design process, we conducted focus groups and think-aloud/co-discovery interview protocol. This involved encouraging interviewees to walk through their actions, expectations, thoughts, and reactions as they navigated our prototype. Verbalizing their thought process helped us gain insight into how we can tweak and tailor our prototypes to be most accessible and useful to our users. During evaluation we asked interviewees to complete three tasks to observe and record the following UX metrics.

UX Evaluation Tasks

  • Find different counseling options and how to schedule counseling
  • Find out how you can get transportation to your appointment at Allina Health
  • Find a peer therapy group going on this Wednesday

UX Metrics

  • Time spent on a given page
  • Error count: number of times a user takes a wrong step to complete the task
  • Number of clicks to get to the desired destination
